# Require recoverable core records and audited restoration

The registry and its audit evidence support child follow-up, official reporting, and accountability, so a corrupted sheet or failed system update must not destroy either current operations or the evidence explaining reported results. Backups will cover child registries, monthly data, activity records, submission confirmations, closed reports, edit and incident logs, proactive and alternative-vaccine data, and necessary account/permission registry data, without retaining readable PINs. A backup point is required before data-structure changes and before monthly close, with regular operational backups to be scheduled according to the chosen tools and workload; restoration is performed by the district system owner or designee, recorded with cause, scope, backup point and verification results, and must be validated before affected submissions or reports are confirmed or released. Recovery capability will be tested before go-live, and lost post-backup field events follow the audited outage fallback path until restored into the system.
